## The colonised HRMS problem

Most enterprise HRMS products treat multi-country as a roadmap item. They are built for one payroll regime, one language, one filing calendar — and then bolt on "international" as an afterthought. For an enterprise running across India, the Gulf, the UK, and the US, that gap becomes spreadsheets, reconciliations, and month-end fire drills.

SanaDeep inverts that. Multi-country is the foundation, not the roadmap. AI is the substrate, not the feature. Compliance is built into the product, not added as documentation.

## AI isn't a feature. It's the substrate.

### 01 · Payroll Anomaly Detection — Catch the error before payroll runs, not after.

SanaDeep's AI reviews every payroll run against historical patterns per employee. It flags calculation errors, duplicate payments, missed statutory deductions, and unusual amounts — before anything is finalized. Your HR team reviews a pre-audit report, not a lawsuit.

→ Flagged: Employee #1842's HRA exemption looks 3× their historical average. Manager approval required before finalization.

### 02 · Predictive Compliance — Know what is changing before it changes you.

SanaDeep monitors labor law and regulatory updates across every country you operate in. When new legislation lands, it predicts the policy updates, cost impact, and deadline — and shows you what to change, where, and by when.

→ Alert: UAE Wage Protection System rule change effective 1 June. 2 policies to update. Estimated configuration time: 15 minutes.

### 03 · Smart Document Parser — Stop keying in data from scanned documents.

Upload a national ID, an offer letter, a visa copy, a medical certificate — our OCR plus NLP engine auto-extracts every relevant field, normalizes formats across languages, and populates the employee record. Confidence scores on each field, manual review for edge cases.

→ Extracted 14 fields from Aadhaar card · Confidence 98% · Auto-populated to employee profile

### 04 · AI Org Designer — The restructure, simulated before it is signed.

Moving a department? Flattening reporting layers? Merging two teams? The AI Org Designer uses performance data, collaboration patterns, and cost signals to suggest optimal structures — and simulates reporting load, communication overhead, and cost changes before you commit.

→ Suggestion: 12 direct reports exceeds recommended span. Proposed split: 2 teams of 6 with intermediate lead.

### 05 · Succession Readiness — The ones who are almost ready, named.

For every leadership and critical role, SanaDeep scores internal candidates on skills, performance trajectory, tenure, and recent development activity. The output is not a replacement plan. It is a shortlist of the people within reach, and the specific gaps to close.

→ 3 candidates ready in 12 months for VP Engineering. Key gap: cross-functional leadership exposure.

### 06 · Team Insights Brief — A monthly digest of what your team actually needs.

Every manager gets an auto-generated brief once a month: tenure distribution, upcoming milestones, at-risk employees, compliance gaps, comp equity flags, org health deltas since last month. Proactive, not reactive. Ten minutes of reading saves four hours of reporting.

→ 2 team members reach probation-end this week · 1 visa expires in 45 days · Comp gap detected in role band D4

## Frequently asked

The questions enterprise buyers always ask us.

### Is SanaDeep truly DPDPA-compliant, or is it "compliance-ready"?

DPDPA compliance is built into the product, not added as documentation. That means field-level consent tracking, automated retention rules, the right to erasure with tombstoning, explicit purpose limitation on AI-processed data, and a complete audit trail of every PII access. Our Compliance Dashboard runs six automated checks continuously and surfaces gaps before they become incidents.

### Can SanaDeep integrate with the systems we already use?

Yes. SanaDeep ships with an Integrations and API Platform module — REST APIs for every data object, webhook support for event streams, and pre-built connectors for common financial systems, ATS tools, identity providers (Google Workspace, SAML SSO, Active Directory on Growth and Enterprise plans), and banking for salary disbursement. Custom integrations are straightforward with our engineering team.

### How long does implementation actually take?

Most Essential and Growth implementations go live in 4 to 6 weeks end-to-end. The first 2 weeks cover data migration and policy configuration, weeks 3 and 4 are manager and employee onboarding with parallel testing, and weeks 5 to 6 are the first parallel payroll run before go-live. Enterprise deployments with multi-entity and cross-country rollouts typically run 8 to 14 weeks.

### We operate across India and the UAE. Does one instance handle both?

One instance, multiple legal entities. Each entity has its own policy framework, payroll engine, statutory filings, and language defaults — while consolidation and reporting happen at the group level. An employee transfer from India to UAE is a native workflow, with data migration and compliance handling automated. This is available on Growth and Enterprise plans.

### Where is our data stored? Can we keep it in India?

By default, Indian customer data is stored in Indian data centers — full stop. For multi-country deployments, we offer regional hosting options so that UAE, UK, or US operations can store data in-region where local regulations require. Enterprise customers can request dedicated infrastructure or on-premise deployments.

### How does the AI actually learn? What happens to our data?

SanaDeep's AI features are tenant-isolated by default. Your data is never used to train shared models or improve the product for other customers. Each AI capability runs against your data alone. For organization-wide benchmarks (such as compensation equity comparisons), we use aggregated and anonymized industry datasets from licensed third-party sources — never customer data.

### What languages does the interface support, and how good is the localization?

Eight interface languages today: English, Hindi, Arabic (with full right-to-left layout), Spanish, French, German, Mandarin, and Portuguese. Localization extends beyond translated labels — date formats, number formats, currency, working week conventions (Sun–Thu in GCC, for example), and statutory form templates all adapt automatically per entity.
