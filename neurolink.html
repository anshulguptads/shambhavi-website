How It Works

From static slides to interactive learning — in four steps.

STEP · 01

Upload

Professors upload PDFs, slides, research papers, or lecture notes. Any format.

STEP · 02

AI Transform

Gemini extracts concepts, generates interactive content, animations, and code playgrounds.

STEP · 03

Students Learn

Personalized lessons, adaptive challenges, 24/7 AI tutor. Each student, their own pace.

STEP · 04

Measure

Real-time analytics on learning gaps, per student and class-wide. Confusion made visible.

Platform Capabilities

Fourteen features. One unified learning graph.

01 · INTELLIGENCE

Concept DNA

A living Neo4j knowledge graph for every student. Not flat scores — interconnected concepts. When CNNs fail, the graph traces back to weak tensor intuition.

02 · RETENTION

Time Machine

Modified SM-2 spaced repetition with AI-adjusted intervals. Daily warmups of 5-10 micro-challenges pulled from last week's concepts.

03 · VISUALIZATION

Animate My Understanding

On-demand 3D visualizations in under 5 seconds. Polished Manim video renders for popular concepts. Sliders to play with parameters.

04 · REASONING

Ghost Mode

Watch the AI solve problems with full reasoning exposed — approach selection, tradeoffs, decisions. Students explore alternate branches at each decision point.

05 · DEBUGGING

Code Archaeology

Cracks open ML black boxes. Captures tensor shapes, gradients, loss values at every checkpoint. DVR-style replay of execution with AI narration.

06 · INQUIRY

Socratic Debug

Never gives the answer. Five graduated hint levels — observation, localization, concept nudge, micro-exercise, explicit fix — configurable by the professor.

07 · COMPETITION

Challenge Arena

Model Bake-Off, Adversarial Debug, Explain-It-To-Win. Competitive coding that teaches intuition, not just syntax.

08 · RESEARCH

Paper to Playground

Upload any ML paper. AI generates an interactive tiered implementation — overview, guided building, experimentation sandbox.

09 · EXPERIMENTATION

Lab Notebook

Auto-logs every execution with hyperparameters, results, and AI analysis. Exports as portfolio-ready documents.

10 · SYNTHESIS

Scenario Simulator

Real-world ML scenarios students walk through — dataset corruption, deployment drift, adversarial attacks. Learn by facing the situation.

11 · CROSS-DOMAIN

Bridges

Maps ML concepts to other domains — gradient descent ↔ supply chain optimization. Parallel visualizations with linked parameters. Perfect for NEP 2020.

12 · COLLABORATION

Study Groups AI

Intelligent group formation based on complementary knowledge gaps. Students learn from peers strategically, not randomly.

13 · ECOSYSTEM

Content Marketplace

Three-tier sharing — department-private, university-commons, global marketplace. Effectiveness scores from anonymized learning data drive discovery.

14 · CAREER

LaunchPad

AI mock interviews personalized from actual projects. System design sandbox. Auto-generated portfolios from Lab Notebook and Challenge Arena.

FAQ

Questions deans ask us.

Do we need to buy GPUs or any new infrastructure?
No. NeuroLink v2 runs entirely on cloud APIs — Google Gemini 3.1 Pro for the AI layer, Neo4j cloud for the knowledge graph, standard web hosting for the application. Universities need a browser. That's it. No GPU procurement, no server maintenance, no capex.
How does this integrate with our existing LMS or academic systems?
NeuroLink can function as a complete standalone platform or as a layer on top of existing LMS systems like Moodle, Canvas, or national platforms like DIKSHA. For government universities, we have a roadmap for integration with UGC and AICTE systems. Authentication supports Google Workspace, SAML SSO, and Active Directory.
Does it only work for CS and AI, or broader fields?
NeuroLink v2 is optimized for Computer Science, Machine Learning, and AI curricula. The Bridges feature explicitly maps ML concepts to other domains, which extends its utility to data-adjacent fields like Operations Research, Computational Biology, and Quantitative Finance. Full support for humanities and pure sciences is on our roadmap.
Where is student data stored? Is it safe?
All data is stored in Indian data centers — data sovereignty is non-negotiable for government institutions. DPDPA-compliant from day one. Student data is tenant-isolated and never used to train shared models. Each institution's data stays with that institution.
What do professors actually have to do differently?
Less, not more. Professors upload their existing lecture materials — slides, PDFs, papers — and the AI transforms them. The Socratic Debug hint levels, the rubric for evaluation, and the challenge types are all configurable, but default settings are designed to require zero setup. Many professors spend less time grading and more time mentoring within the first month.
Can we run this for a single department before a university-wide rollout?
Yes, and we strongly recommend it. Our standard pilot structure starts with a single CS or AI department, one semester, roughly 200–500 students. Success is measured against a control cohort using standard assessments. Scale-up decisions happen based on evidence, not enthusiasm.
